\section{Question No. 2.53}
Solve x+y $\leq$ 9,y $>$ x,x $\geq$ 0
\section{SOLUTION}
Let x+y=9 intersects the x-axis and y-axis at $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ respectively.Let,
\begin{align}
\vec{A}=\myvec{x\\0}
\implies x=9
\implies \vec{A}=\myvec{9\\0}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\vec{B}=\myvec{0\\y}
\implies y=9\implies\vec{B}=\myvec{0\\9}
\end{align}
Origin&=\myvec{0\\0} does satisfy the equation x+y$\leq$9\\The solution is the left side of the line x+y=9.
\\
Now,let y=x intersects the x-axis and y-axis at C and D respectively.Let,
\begin{align}
\vec{C}=\myvec{0\\y}\implies y=0\implies \vec{C}=\myvec{0\\0}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\vec{D}=\myvec{2\\y}\implies y=2\implies \vec{D}=\myvec{2\\2}
\end{align}
Origin&=\myvec{9\\0} does not satisfy the equation x$>$y\\The solution is the left side of the line x=y\\
Also,x$\geq$0.So,the solution is the right side of y-axis.
\end{enumerate}
